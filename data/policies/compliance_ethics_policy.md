# Perficient Compliance and Ethics Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Last Updated:** March 15, 2024  
**Policy Owner:** Chief Ethics & Compliance Officer  
**Applies To:** All Perficient directors, officers, employees, and contractors worldwide

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes Perficient's compliance and ethics framework, ensuring the company operates with integrity in all business dealings and meets its regulatory obligations. Ethical behavior is not optional — it is foundational to our reputation, client trust, and long-term success.

---

- Risk assessment and monitoring

- Investigation and response procedures

## 3. Regulatory Compliance

### 3.1 Securities Laws
As a publicly traded company (NASDAQ: PRFT), Perficient must comply with:
- Securities Exchange Act of 1934 (SEC reporting, proxy rules)
- Sarbanes-Oxley Act (SOX) — internal controls, financial reporting accuracy
- Insider trading prohibitions (see Section 5)
- Regulation FD (fair disclosure)

### 3.2 Anti-Corruption Laws
- Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA) — US
- UK Bribery Act — international operations
- State and local anti-corruption statutes
- Zero tolerance for bribery in any form (see Code of Conduct)

### 3.3 Data and Privacy Regulations
- GDPR (European Union)
- CCPA/CPRA (California)
- HIPAA (healthcare data, where applicable)
- State privacy laws (Virginia CDPA, Colorado CPA, Connecticut CTDPA)
- Industry-specific: PCI DSS (payment cards), FERPA (education), GLBA (financial)

### 3.4 Employment Laws
- Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA)
- Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA)
- Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)
- Title VII of the Civil Rights Act
- Age Discrimination in Employment Act (ADEA)
- State and local employment laws

### 3.5 Export Controls
- Export Administration Regulations (EAR)
- Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) sanctions
- International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR) — if applicable
- Screen all international clients and vendors against denied party lists

## 4. Ethics Standards

### 4.1 Business Integrity
All employees must:
- Conduct business honestly and transparently
- Honor commitments and contractual obligations
- Compete fairly (no collusion, bid-rigging, or market manipulation)
- Maintain accurate records and reports
- Report ethical concerns without fear of retaliation

### 4.2 Financial Integrity
- All financial records must be accurate, complete, and timely
- No hidden accounts, off-balance-sheet arrangements, or unrecorded transactions
- Expense reports must reflect actual business expenses
- Revenue recognition follows GAAP and company policies
- Internal controls must not be circumvented for any reason

### 4.3 Client Integrity
- Deliver what we promise (scope, timeline, quality)
- Disclose conflicts of interest before engagement
- Protect client confidential information
- Bill honestly and accurately
- Do not disparage clients (current or former)
- Decline engagements that conflict with ethical standards

## 5. Insider Trading Policy

### 5.1 Material Non-Public Information (MNPI)
Employees who possess MNPI about Perficient may NOT:
- Buy or sell Perficient stock (PRFT)
- Buy or sell options on Perficient stock
- Tip others (family, friends, colleagues) about the information
- Trade in securities of clients or partners based on MNPI gained through Perficient engagement

### 5.2 Examples of MNPI
- Earnings results before public release
- Pending mergers, acquisitions, or partnerships
- Major client wins or losses not yet announced
- Executive changes not yet announced
- Material legal proceedings
- Product launches or strategic shifts

### 5.3 Trading Windows
- **Blackout periods:** Trading prohibited from 2 weeks before quarter-end until 2 business days after earnings release
- **Pre-clearance:** Officers and directors must pre-clear all trades with Legal
- **10b5-1 plans:** Available for systematic trading (requires Legal setup)

### 5.4 Consequences
Insider trading violations carry:
- Immediate termination
- Civil penalties up to 3x profits gained or losses avoided
- Criminal penalties up to $5 million and 20 years imprisonment
- SEC enforcement actions

## 6. Conflicts of Interest

### 6.1 Definition
A conflict exists when personal interests compete with professional obligations to Perficient or its clients.

### 6.2 Common Conflicts
- Financial interest in a client, vendor, or competitor
- Outside employment with a competitor or client
- Hiring or supervising family members
- Personal relationships with clients or vendors that influence business decisions
- Board memberships that conflict with Perficient's interests
- Investment in companies Perficient may acquire

### 6.3 Disclosure and Management
- All potential conflicts must be disclosed via the Annual Conflict of Interest Questionnaire (due January 31)
- New conflicts arising mid-year must be disclosed within 10 business days
- Compliance team evaluates and recommends mitigation (recusal, divestiture, monitoring)
- Undisclosed conflicts discovered through audit may result in disciplinary action

## 7. Reporting and Whistleblower Protection

### 7.1 Reporting Channels
Report compliance or ethics concerns through:
- Direct manager or skip-level manager
- HR Business Partner
- Chief Ethics & Compliance Officer (ethics@perficient.com)
- Anonymous Ethics Hotline: 1-800-PRFT-ETH (1-800-773-8384)
- Online portal: perficient.ethicspoint.com
- Board Audit Committee (for concerns about senior executives)

### 7.2 Anonymity
- Ethics Hotline and online portal allow anonymous reporting
- Reporters are never required to identify themselves
- If identity is known, it is kept confidential to the extent possible
- Investigation outcomes shared with reporter when identity is known

### 7.3 Non-Retaliation Guarantee
Perficient strictly prohibits retaliation against anyone who:
- Reports a concern in good faith
- Participates in an investigation
- Refuses to engage in unethical behavior
- Raises concerns about potential violations

Retaliation includes: termination, demotion, harassment, intimidation, pay reduction, undesirable reassignment, or any other adverse action.

Retaliation is itself a serious violation, subject to disciplinary action up to and including termination.

### 7.4 Bad Faith Reports
Knowingly false reports made with malicious intent are themselves policy violations. However, a report made in good faith is protected even if the investigation does not substantiate the concern.

## 8. Investigations

### 8.1 Process
All reports are investigated following a standard process:
1. Receipt acknowledgment (within 2 business days)
2. Preliminary assessment (within 5 business days)
3. Full investigation if warranted (assigned investigator)
4. Interviews, document review, forensic analysis as needed
5. Findings documented with supporting evidence
6. Determination and recommended action
7. Resolution and communication to affected parties
8. Monitoring and follow-up

### 8.2 Cooperation
All employees must cooperate fully with compliance investigations:
- Respond to interview requests promptly
- Provide requested documents and information
- Do not destroy evidence or obstruct investigations
- Maintain confidentiality about ongoing investigations
- Failure to cooperate is itself a policy violation

### 8.3 Outcomes
Investigation outcomes may include:
- No violation found (case closed, reporter informed)
- Policy violation confirmed → disciplinary action
- Criminal activity identified → referral to law enforcement
- Systemic issue identified → policy or process improvement
- Training gap identified → targeted education program
