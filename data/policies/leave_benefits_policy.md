# Perficient Leave and Benefits Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Last Updated:** January 1, 2024  
**Policy Owner:** VP of Total Rewards  
**Applies To:** All full-time Perficient employees (part-time employees receive prorated benefits)

---

## 1. Paid Time Off (PTO)

### 1.1 PTO Accrual Schedule

| Tenure | Annual PTO Days | Accrual Rate (per pay period) |
|--------|----------------|-------------------------------|
| 0-2 years | 18 days | 5.54 hours |
| 3-5 years | 22 days | 6.77 hours |
| 6-10 years | 26 days | 8.00 hours |
| 11+ years | 30 days | 9.23 hours |

### 1.2 PTO Guidelines
- PTO begins accruing from the first day of employment
- Maximum carryover: 5 days into the next calendar year (use-it-or-lose-it for excess)
- PTO requests must be submitted in Workday at least 5 business days in advance for planned absences
- Manager approval is required; managers should respond within 2 business days
- PTO is paid at the employee's regular base rate
- Upon separation, accrued unused PTO is paid out (up to maximum carryover amount)

### 1.3 Blackout Periods
Certain teams may designate blackout periods during critical business cycles (e.g., year-end close for Finance). Blackout periods must be communicated at least 30 days in advance.

## 3. Sick Leave

### 3.1 Sick Time
- Full-time employees receive 10 sick days per year (80 hours), front-loaded on January 1
- Sick time may be used for personal illness, medical appointments, or caring for an ill family member
- Doctor's note required for absences exceeding 3 consecutive days
- Unused sick time carries over up to 30 days maximum (for future FMLA qualification)
- Sick time is not paid out upon separation

### 3.2 Extended Illness
For illnesses exceeding 5 consecutive business days, employees should contact HR to discuss:
- Short-term disability eligibility
- FMLA qualification
- Return-to-work documentation requirements

## 4. Parental Leave

### 4.1 Birth Parents
- 16 weeks of fully-paid parental leave
- Additional 6 weeks at 60% pay available (short-term disability)
- Leave begins on the date of birth or C-section
- May be taken continuously or in blocks (with manager agreement)

### 4.2 Non-Birth Parents (Partners, Adoptive, Foster Parents)
- 12 weeks of fully-paid parental leave
- Leave must begin within 12 months of birth/adoption/placement
- May be taken in 1-week minimum increments

### 4.3 Eligibility
Parental leave is available to employees who:
- Have completed 6 months of employment
- Are in good standing (no active PIP)
- Provide 30 days' advance notice when possible

### 4.4 Benefits During Leave
All benefits (health insurance, life insurance, 401k) continue during parental leave. The company continues its portion of premium payments.

## 5. Family and Medical Leave (FMLA)

### 5.1 Eligibility
Employees who have worked at Perficient for at least 12 months and 1,250 hours are eligible for up to 12 weeks of FMLA leave per rolling 12-month period.

### 5.2 Qualifying Reasons
- Serious health condition of the employee
- Caring for a family member (spouse, child, parent) with a serious health condition
- Birth and bonding with a new child
- Adoption or foster care placement
- Military caregiver leave (up to 26 weeks)
- Qualifying exigency related to military deployment

### 5.3 FMLA Process
1. Notify HR and manager as soon as the need is foreseeable
2. Complete FMLA request form in Workday
3. Provide medical certification within 15 days
4. HR will designate the leave and notify of rights
5. Maintain communication with HR during leave
6. Provide fitness-for-duty certification before return (if applicable)

## 6. Other Leave Types

### 6.1 Bereavement Leave
- Immediate family (spouse, child, parent, sibling): 5 paid days
- Extended family (grandparent, in-law, aunt/uncle): 3 paid days
- Close friend or colleague: 1 paid day
- Additional unpaid time may be approved by manager

### 6.2 Jury Duty
Perficient provides full pay for the duration of jury duty. Provide the jury summons to HR and your manager. If dismissed early, return to work for the remainder of the day.

### 6.3 Voting Leave
Up to 2 hours of paid time to vote on Election Day if polls are not open for 2+ hours outside of working hours.

### 6.4 Military Leave
Leave for active military duty or training is provided per USERRA requirements. Differential pay (difference between military and Perficient pay) is provided for up to 12 months. Benefits continue for up to 24 months.

### 6.5 Sabbatical
Employees with 7+ years of continuous service may request a 4-week paid sabbatical. Sabbaticals require VP approval and 90 days' advance notice. Only one sabbatical per 7-year period.

## 7. Health Insurance

### 7.1 Medical Plans
Perficient offers three medical plan options through UnitedHealthcare:
- **PPO Plan:** Broader network, higher premiums, lower deductible ($500 individual/$1,000 family)
- **HDHP Plan:** Lower premiums, higher deductible ($1,500 individual/$3,000 family), HSA-eligible
- **HMO Plan:** Lowest premiums, requires PCP referrals, limited to in-network providers

### 7.2 Dental
Delta Dental PPO plan covering:
- Preventive (cleanings, x-rays): 100% covered
- Basic (fillings, extractions): 80% covered
- Major (crowns, bridges): 50% covered
- Annual maximum: $2,000 per person
- Orthodontia: 50% up to $2,000 lifetime maximum

### 7.3 Vision
VSP Vision plan covering:
- Annual eye exam: $10 copay
- Frames: $200 allowance every 2 years
- Lenses: Covered in full annually
- Contact lens allowance: $150/year

### 7.4 Premiums
Perficient covers 80% of employee-only premiums and 65% of dependent premiums. Premium costs are deducted pre-tax from each paycheck.

### 7.5 Enrollment
- New hires: Must enroll within 30 days of start date
- Open enrollment: November 1-15 each year for January 1 effective date
- Qualifying life events: Marriage, birth/adoption, divorce, loss of other coverage — 30-day enrollment window

## 8. Retirement Benefits

### 8.1 401(k) Plan
- Eligible from Day 1 of employment
- Employee contributions: Up to IRS annual limit ($23,000 for 2024; $30,500 for employees 50+)
- Company match: 50% of employee contributions up to 6% of salary (effective maximum: 3% company contribution)
- Vesting: Company match vests over 3 years (33%/66%/100%)
- Investment options: 25+ mutual funds, target-date funds, self-directed brokerage window

### 8.2 Employee Stock Purchase Plan (ESPP)
- Purchase Perficient stock (PRFT) at 15% discount
- Contribution: 1-15% of base salary per pay period
- Purchase periods: Semi-annual (June 30, December 31)
- Eligibility: After 6 months of employment

## 9. Additional Benefits

### 9.1 Life Insurance
- Basic: 2x annual salary (company-paid, up to $500,000)
- Supplemental: Up to 5x salary available at employee cost
- Spouse/dependent: Available at group rates

### 9.2 Disability Insurance
- Short-term disability: 60% of salary for up to 26 weeks (company-paid)
- Long-term disability: 60% of salary after 26 weeks (company-paid, up to $15,000/month)

### 9.3 Employee Assistance Program (EAP)
- 6 free counseling sessions per year per issue
- Available to employees and household members
- Covers: Mental health, substance abuse, financial counseling, legal consultation
- 24/7 crisis hotline: 1-800-EAP-PRFT

### 9.4 Wellness Program
- $600/year wellness reimbursement (gym, fitness classes, wellness apps)
- Annual biometric screening (optional, earns $200 HSA contribution)
- Virtual health coaching
- Meditation/mindfulness app subscription (Headspace)

### 9.5 Other Perks
- Commuter benefits (pre-tax transit/parking up to IRS limits)
- Pet insurance (voluntary, group rates)
- Identity theft protection (company-provided)
- Legal plan (voluntary, MetLife Legal)
- Adoption assistance: Up to $5,000 per adoption
