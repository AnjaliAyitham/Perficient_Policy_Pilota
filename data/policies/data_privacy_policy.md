# Perficient Data Privacy Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Last Updated:** April 15, 2024  
**Policy Owner:** Data Protection Officer (DPO)  
**Applies To:** All Perficient employees who collect, process, store, or access personal data

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes Perficient's commitment to protecting the privacy of personal data belonging to employees, clients, prospects, and third parties. Perficient complies with applicable privacy regulations including GDPR, CCPA/CPRA, HIPAA (where applicable), and state privacy laws.

---

## 2. Definitions

- **Personal Data (PD):** Any information that identifies or can identify a natural person (name, email, phone, address, IP address, employee ID)
- **Sensitive Personal Data (SPD):** Health information, biometric data, racial/ethnic origin, religious beliefs, sexual orientation, financial account numbers, Social Security numbers
- **Data Subject:** The individual whose personal data is being processed
- **Data Controller:** Perficient, when we determine the purposes and means of processing
- **Data Processor:** Perficient, when we process data on behalf of our clients
- **Processing:** Any operation performed on personal data (collection, storage, use, sharing, deletion)

---

## 3. Data Collection Principles

### 3.1 Lawful Basis
Personal data may only be collected and processed when there is a valid legal basis:
- **Consent:** The individual has given explicit consent
- **Contract:** Processing is necessary to fulfill a contract
- **Legal obligation:** Required by law or regulation
- **Legitimate interest:** Perficient has a legitimate business interest that is balanced against the individual's rights

### 3.2 Data Minimization
Collect only the minimum personal data necessary for the stated purpose. Do not collect "nice-to-have" data. Review data collection forms and processes annually to ensure minimization.

### 3.3 Purpose Limitation
Personal data collected for one purpose must not be used for a different, incompatible purpose without additional consent or legal basis.

---

## 4. Data Handling Requirements

### 4.1 Employee Data
HR collects and processes employee data for:
- Employment administration (payroll, benefits, taxation)
- Performance management
- Legal compliance (I-9, background checks)
- Business operations (directory, org chart)

Employee data is stored in Workday (HRIS) and accessible only to HR, the employee's management chain, and authorized personnel.

### 4.2 Client Data
When processing client data:
- Follow the Data Processing Agreement (DPA) for each client engagement
- Access only the data necessary for your project role
- Never copy client data to personal devices or unauthorized systems
- Report any unauthorized access immediately
- Delete or return client data at project completion per the DPA terms

### 4.3 Prospect and Marketing Data
Marketing data (prospect names, emails, company info) is processed under legitimate interest for:
- Email marketing (with opt-out capability)
- Event invitations
- Sales outreach

All marketing emails must include an unsubscribe mechanism. Honor opt-out requests within 10 business days.

---

## 5. Data Storage and Retention

### 5.1 Storage Requirements
Personal data must be stored in approved, secure systems:
- **Employee data:** Workday, secured SharePoint
- **Client project data:** Client-approved environments (Azure, AWS per SOW)
- **Marketing data:** Salesforce, HubSpot (approved instances)
- **Financial data:** NetSuite, secured finance SharePoint

### 5.2 Prohibited Storage Locations
Personal data must NEVER be stored in:
- Personal email accounts
- Personal cloud storage (Google Drive, personal Dropbox)
- Unencrypted USB drives
- Local desktop files (outside managed OneDrive)
- Shared drives without access controls
- Communication tools (pasting PII in Slack/Teams messages)

### 5.3 Retention Periods

| Data Type | Retention Period | Basis |
|-----------|-----------------|-------|
| Employee records | 7 years after separation | Legal/tax compliance |
| Client project data | Per DPA (typically 1-3 years post-engagement) | Contractual |
| Recruitment data | 2 years from application date | Legitimate interest |
| Financial records | 7 years | Tax/regulatory |
| Marketing consent records | Duration of consent + 3 years | GDPR/CCPA |
| Security logs | 1 year | Security operations |

### 5.4 Data Deletion
When retention periods expire, data must be securely deleted:
- Digital: Secure delete/crypto-shred from all systems including backups
- Physical: Cross-cut shredding
- Confirm deletion with the Data Privacy team

## 6. Data Subject Rights

### 6.1 Rights Under GDPR/CCPA
Data subjects have the right to:
- **Access:** Request a copy of their personal data
- **Rectification:** Correct inaccurate data
- **Erasure:** Request deletion ("right to be forgotten")
- **Portability:** Receive data in a machine-readable format
- **Objection:** Object to processing based on legitimate interest
- **Restriction:** Limit how their data is used
- **Opt-out of sale:** Decline sale/sharing of personal data (CCPA)

### 6.2 Responding to Requests
All data subject requests must be:
- Forwarded to privacy@perficient.com immediately upon receipt
- Acknowledged within 3 business days
- Fulfilled within 30 days (GDPR) or 45 days (CCPA)
- Documented in the privacy request log

### 6.3 Employee Requests
Employees seeking access to their own data should contact HR. Requests to delete employee data are subject to legal retention requirements.

## 7. Data Breach Procedures

### 7.1 What Constitutes a Breach
A personal data breach is any incident involving:
- Unauthorized access to personal data
- Accidental disclosure (sending data to the wrong recipient)
- Loss of data (lost device with unencrypted PD)
- Theft of data (cyberattack)
- Unauthorized alteration of personal data

### 7.2 Reporting
Report suspected breaches immediately:
- **Internal:** security@perficient.com and privacy@perficient.com
- **After hours:** Security Operations Center ext. 9111

### 7.3 Notification Requirements
- **GDPR:** Supervisory authority notified within 72 hours if risk to individuals
- **CCPA:** Affected individuals notified without unreasonable delay
- **Client notification:** Per DPA terms (typically 24-72 hours)
- **Perficient employees:** Notified directly if their data is affected

### 7.4 Breach Response Steps
1. Contain the breach (stop the leak)
2. Assess scope and severity
3. Notify required parties per regulatory timelines
4. Investigate root cause
5. Remediate vulnerabilities
6. Document and report lessons learned

---

## 8. Cross-Border Data Transfers

### 8.1 International Transfers
Transferring personal data outside the originating country requires:
- Adequate safeguards (Standard Contractual Clauses, adequacy decisions)
- Transfer Impact Assessment for high-risk jurisdictions
- DPO approval for new transfer mechanisms
- Client consent where required by DPA

### 8.2 US-EU Transfers
Perficient relies on Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) for EU-to-US data transfers. All relevant vendor contracts must include SCCs.

## 9. Training and Compliance

### 9.1 Mandatory Training
- Annual Data Privacy Awareness training (all employees, due by March 31)
- Role-specific training for teams handling sensitive data (HR, Finance, Client Services)
- Privacy-by-design training for engineering teams

### 9.2 Privacy Impact Assessments (PIAs)
A PIA is required before:
- Launching new systems that process personal data
- Changing how existing personal data is used
- Engaging new vendors with access to personal data
- Starting client projects involving PII/SPD

### 9.3 Compliance Monitoring
The Data Privacy team conducts:
- Quarterly access reviews of sensitive data systems
- Annual privacy audits
- Random compliance spot-checks
- Vendor privacy assessment reviews
