# Perficient Performance Review Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Last Updated:** February 1, 2024  
**Policy Owner:** VP of Talent Management  
**Applies To:** All Perficient employees (full-time and part-time)

---

## 1. Performance Management Philosophy

Perficient believes in continuous feedback, development-focused conversations, and transparent performance expectations. Our performance management process is designed to:
- Align individual goals with company strategy
- Provide regular, actionable feedback
- Support career development and growth
- Inform compensation and promotion decisions
- Identify and develop high-potential talent

---

## 2. Performance Cycle

### 2.1 Annual Timeline

| Period | Activity |
|--------|----------|
| January | Goal setting for the new year |
| March | Q1 check-in (informal) |
| June | Mid-year review (formal, documented) |
| September | Q3 check-in (informal) |
| November-December | Annual review (formal, rating assigned) |
| February-March | Compensation adjustments effective |

### 2.2 Continuous Feedback
In addition to formal reviews, managers and employees should engage in:
- Weekly 1:1 meetings (30 minutes minimum)
- Real-time feedback on specific deliverables
- Peer feedback through the Recognition platform
- Post-project retrospectives

## 3. Goal Setting

### 3.1 Goal Framework
Perficient uses the OKR (Objectives and Key Results) framework:
- **Objectives:** 3-5 qualitative goals aligned with team and company priorities
- **Key Results:** 2-4 measurable outcomes per objective
- Goals should be ambitious but achievable (70% target completion = success)

### 3.2 Goal Categories
Each employee should have goals across these dimensions:
- **Business Impact (50%):** Client delivery, revenue contribution, project outcomes
- **Technical/Professional Growth (30%):** Skills development, certifications, knowledge sharing
- **Leadership & Collaboration (20%):** Mentoring, team contribution, culture building

### 3.3 Goal Setting Process
1. Company announces annual priorities (January)
2. Managers cascade team objectives
3. Employees draft individual OKRs
4. Manager and employee align on goals in 1:1
5. Goals documented in Workday by January 31
6. Goals may be adjusted at mid-year with manager agreement

## 4. Performance Rating Scale

### 4.1 Rating Definitions

| Rating | Label | Description |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| 5 | Exceptional | Consistently exceeds all expectations; role model performance; transformative impact |
| 4 | Exceeds Expectations | Regularly exceeds expectations; strong, consistent performance above standard |
| 3 | Meets Expectations | Consistently meets all job requirements; solid, reliable performer |
| 2 | Needs Improvement | Falls short of expectations in some areas; development plan required |
| 1 | Unsatisfactory | Consistently fails to meet basic job requirements; immediate action required |

### 4.2 Rating Distribution (Guideline)
Managers should aim for calibrated distribution:
- Rating 5: ~5% of team
- Rating 4: ~25% of team
- Rating 3: ~55% of team
- Rating 2: ~12% of team
- Rating 1: ~3% of team

Note: This is a guideline, not a forced distribution. Actual ratings should reflect true performance.

### 4.3 Calibration
Performance ratings are calibrated across teams and business units to ensure consistency. Calibration sessions occur in December with:
- Managers presenting ratings for their teams
- VP-level review for cross-team equity
- HR facilitation to address potential bias

## 5. Mid-Year Review

### 5.1 Purpose
The mid-year review is a formal checkpoint to:
- Assess progress toward annual goals
- Provide structured feedback
- Adjust goals if business priorities have changed
- Identify development needs early
- Prevent year-end surprises

### 5.2 Process
1. Employee completes self-assessment in Workday (June 1-15)
2. Manager completes assessment (June 15-30)
3. Manager and employee have review conversation (by July 15)
4. Both parties document conversation and action items in Workday

### 5.3 No Rating at Mid-Year
The mid-year review is qualitative feedback only — no performance rating is assigned. This encourages open, developmental conversation.

---

## 6. Annual Review

### 6.1 Process Timeline
- **November 1-15:** Employee completes self-assessment
- **November 1-15:** 360 feedback collected (peer, cross-functional, client)
- **November 15-30:** Manager completes assessment and proposes rating
- **December 1-15:** Calibration sessions
- **December 15-31:** Manager delivers final rating in review conversation
- **January 1-15:** Ratings finalized in Workday

### 6.2 Self-Assessment
Employees should document:
- Key accomplishments against each OKR
- Challenges encountered and how they were addressed
- Skills developed and certifications earned
- Contributions to team and culture
- Goals and development priorities for next year

### 6.3 360 Feedback
- Each employee nominates 3-5 peers for feedback
- Manager may add additional reviewers
- Feedback is confidential (aggregated themes shared, not attributed quotes)
- Focus areas: collaboration, communication, technical skills, leadership

## 7. Promotion Process

### 7.1 Promotion Criteria
Promotions are based on:
- Sustained performance at the next level (not just exceeding current level)
- Demonstrated readiness across all competencies for the target level
- Business need and headcount availability
- Minimum 12 months in current role (exceptions for extraordinary circumstances)

### 7.2 Promotion Cycle
- **Primary cycle:** Annual (effective March 1)
- **Off-cycle promotions:** Quarterly, for retention-critical situations (requires VP + HR approval)

### 7.3 Promotion Process
1. Manager nominates employee with promotion justification
2. Skip-level manager reviews and approves
3. HR validates against level competencies
4. Calibration with peer group
5. Final approval by BU leader
6. Employee notified with new title, compensation, and expectations

## 8. Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs)

### 8.1 When PIPs Are Used
A PIP is initiated when an employee:
- Receives a rating of 2 (Needs Improvement) or 1 (Unsatisfactory)
- Has received documented verbal and written feedback without improvement
- Is not meeting core job requirements despite support

### 8.2 PIP Structure
PIPs are typically 30-60-90 days and include:
- Specific, measurable performance expectations
- Resources and support available (training, mentoring, reduced workload)
- Weekly check-in schedule with manager
- Clear consequences of not meeting expectations
- HR Business Partner involvement throughout

### 8.3 PIP Outcomes
At PIP conclusion:
- **Successful:** Employee demonstrates sustained improvement; PIP closes; normal performance management resumes
- **Partial improvement:** PIP may be extended once (up to 30 additional days)
- **Unsuccessful:** Employment may be terminated per HR and Legal guidance

### 8.4 Employee Rights During PIP
- Written documentation of all expectations and feedback
- Regular check-ins and coaching
- Access to EAP and HR support
- Ability to contest PIP terms with HR Business Partner
- No retaliation for raising concerns about the PIP process

## 9. Career Development

### 9.1 Career Ladders
Perficient maintains defined career ladders for each function:
- **Technical track:** Engineer → Senior Engineer → Staff Engineer → Principal Engineer → Distinguished Engineer
- **Management track:** Team Lead → Manager → Senior Manager → Director → VP
- **Consulting track:** Consultant → Senior Consultant → Managing Consultant → Director → Principal

### 9.2 Individual Development Plans (IDPs)
Every employee should maintain an IDP that includes:
- Long-term career aspiration (3-5 year vision)
- Skills to develop in the next 12 months
- Specific actions (courses, certifications, stretch assignments, mentoring)
- Manager support needed
- Timeline and milestones

### 9.3 Development Resources
- Annual $2,500 training budget per employee
- Internal learning platform (Perficient Academy)
- Mentorship program (formal matching twice per year)
- Leadership development programs (nominated by VP)
- Cross-functional project opportunities
- Conference speaking and attendance
