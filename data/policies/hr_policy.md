# Perficient Human Resources Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Last Updated:** March 15, 2024  
**Policy Owner:** Chief People Officer  
**Applies To:** All Perficient employees (full-time, part-time, and contract)

---

## 1. Employment Classifications

### 1.1 Full-Time Employees
Full-time employees work a minimum of 40 hours per week and are eligible for all company benefits including health insurance, 401(k), PTO, and stock purchase programs.

### 1.2 Part-Time Employees
Part-time employees work fewer than 30 hours per week. They are eligible for limited benefits as outlined in the Benefits Summary document.

### 1.3 Contract Employees
Contract employees are engaged through Perficient's staffing partners for project-based work. They are not eligible for Perficient benefits but must adhere to all workplace conduct policies.

### 1.4 Interns
Perficient offers paid internship programs lasting 10-12 weeks. Interns are assigned mentors and participate in structured learning programs. Intern compensation is determined by role, location, and program level.

---

## 2. Hiring and Onboarding

### 2.1 Hiring Process
All positions must be approved by the hiring manager's VP and the Talent Acquisition team. The standard hiring process includes:
- Job requisition approval
- Resume screening by Talent Acquisition
- Phone screen (30 minutes)
- Technical assessment or case study (role-dependent)
- Panel interview (2-3 interviewers)
- Final interview with hiring manager
- Reference checks (2 professional references)
- Offer letter and background check

### 2.2 Background Checks
All offers are contingent on successful completion of background checks including criminal history, employment verification, and education verification. Certain client-facing roles may require additional security clearance.

### 2.3 Onboarding
New hires complete a structured 2-week onboarding program that includes:
- Day 1: IT setup, badge issuance, benefits enrollment, HR orientation
- Week 1: Company culture sessions, compliance training, team introductions
- Week 2: Project assignment, mentor pairing, role-specific training
- 30/60/90-day check-ins with manager and HR Business Partner

---

## 3. Working Hours and Attendance

### 3.1 Standard Working Hours
Core business hours are 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM local time, Monday through Friday. Employees may adjust their schedules with manager approval, provided they maintain 40 hours per week and are available during core collaboration hours (10:00 AM - 3:00 PM local time).

### 3.2 Overtime
Non-exempt employees are eligible for overtime pay at 1.5x their regular hourly rate for hours exceeding 40 per week. All overtime must be pre-approved by the direct manager.

### 3.3 Attendance
Employees are expected to maintain consistent attendance. Unplanned absences should be communicated to the direct manager before the start of the workday. Three or more consecutive unplanned absences require a doctor's note.

---

## 4. Workplace Conduct

### 4.1 Professional Behavior
All employees are expected to conduct themselves professionally, treating colleagues, clients, and partners with respect and courtesy. This includes both in-person and virtual interactions.

### 4.2 Anti-Harassment Policy
Perficient maintains a zero-tolerance policy toward harassment of any kind, including but not limited to:
- Sexual harassment
- Racial or ethnic discrimination
- Age discrimination
- Disability discrimination
- Religious discrimination
- Gender identity or sexual orientation discrimination

Any employee who experiences or witnesses harassment should report it immediately to their manager, HR Business Partner, or through the anonymous Ethics Hotline at 1-800-PRFT-ETH.

### 4.3 Workplace Violence
Threats of violence, intimidation, or aggressive behavior are prohibited. Employees who feel unsafe should contact Security immediately and report the incident to HR.

### 4.4 Substance Abuse
Perficient maintains a drug-free workplace. The use, possession, or distribution of illegal substances on company premises or while conducting company business is prohibited and grounds for immediate termination.

---

## 5. Compensation and Payroll

### 5.1 Pay Schedule
Employees are paid bi-weekly on Fridays. Direct deposit is required for all employees. Pay stubs are available through the Workday portal.

### 5.2 Annual Reviews and Merit Increases
Compensation reviews occur annually in Q1 (February-March). Merit increases are based on performance ratings, market data, and budget availability. The typical merit increase range is 3-8% for meets/exceeds expectations ratings.

### 5.3 Bonus Programs
- **Annual Performance Bonus:** Eligible employees may receive 5-20% of base salary based on individual and company performance.
- **Spot Bonuses:** Managers can award $500-$2,500 spot bonuses for exceptional contributions.
- **Referral Bonuses:** $3,000-$7,500 for successful employee referrals (paid after the referred employee's 90-day anniversary).

---

## 6. Employee Development

### 6.1 Training Budget
Each employee receives a $2,500 annual training budget for professional development including conferences, certifications, courses, and books. Additional funding may be approved by the direct manager's VP.

### 6.2 Certification Support
Perficient covers the cost of role-relevant certifications (exam fees and study materials). Employees who pass certifications receive a $500 bonus per certification.

### 6.3 Tuition Reimbursement
Full-time employees with 1+ year of tenure are eligible for tuition reimbursement up to $5,250 per calendar year for degree programs relevant to their role.

---

## 7. Separation and Offboarding

### 7.1 Voluntary Resignation
Employees are requested to provide a minimum of 2 weeks' written notice. Managers and directors should provide 4 weeks' notice. Resignation notices should be submitted to the direct manager and HR Business Partner.

### 7.2 Involuntary Termination
Terminations for cause require documentation of policy violations and approval from the HR Business Partner and Legal team. Employees terminated for cause are not eligible for severance.

### 7.3 Severance
Employees terminated due to restructuring or position elimination may be eligible for severance based on tenure: 2 weeks of base pay per year of service, with a minimum of 4 weeks and maximum of 26 weeks.

### 7.4 Exit Process
All departing employees must complete:
- Equipment return (laptop, badge, company phone)
- Knowledge transfer documentation
- Exit interview with HR
- Final expense report submission within 5 business days
