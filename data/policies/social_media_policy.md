# Perficient Social Media Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Last Updated:** January 30, 2024  
**Policy Owner:** VP of Marketing & Communications  
**Applies To:** All Perficient employees in their professional and personal social media activities

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy provides guidelines for employees' use of social media, both in their professional capacity representing Perficient and in personal use that may reflect on the company. Perficient encourages employees to be active thought leaders while protecting company and client interests.

---

## 2. Official Perficient Social Media

### 2.1 Authorized Accounts
Only the Marketing & Communications team manages official Perficient social media accounts:
- LinkedIn: /company/perficient
- Twitter/X: @Perficient
- YouTube: Perficient Inc
- Instagram: @perficient
- Facebook: /Perficient
- GitHub: /Perficient

### 2.2 Who May Post Officially
- Marketing team members (as part of their role)
- Executives with Communications team support
- Employees participating in approved advocacy programs
- Agency partners under Marketing supervision

### 2.3 Content Approval
All content posted to official Perficient channels must be:
- Reviewed by the Marketing team prior to posting
- Aligned with brand guidelines and messaging
- Free of client confidential information
- Compliant with regulatory requirements
- Accessible (alt text, captions for videos)

---

## 3. Employee Personal Social Media

### 3.1 General Guidelines
When posting on personal social media:
- Be authentic and respectful
- Remember that your posts are public and permanent
- Consider how your content reflects on Perficient
- When in doubt, don't post — or consult Marketing first

### 3.2 Required Disclosures
If you mention Perficient or discuss topics related to your work:
- Include "Views are my own" or similar disclaimer in your bio
- Disclose your affiliation with Perficient if commenting on industry topics
- Use #ad or appropriate disclosure for sponsored content

### 3.3 Permitted Activities
Employees are encouraged to:
- Share official Perficient content with personal commentary
- Write about professional expertise and industry trends
- Engage in professional networking
- Participate in technical communities and forums
- Share conference experiences and learning
- Celebrate team and company achievements

### 3.4 Prohibited Activities
Employees must NOT:
- Disclose confidential company or client information
- Share proprietary code, architectures, or methodologies without approval
- Disparage clients, partners, competitors, or colleagues
- Post content that could be interpreted as the official Perficient position
- Engage in heated arguments or "flame wars" that reflect poorly on Perficient
- Share screenshots of internal tools, Slack messages, or emails
- Post about unannounced products, partnerships, or financial information
- Use Perficient logos or branded assets without Marketing approval

---

## 4. Thought Leadership Program

### 4.1 Perficient Voices Program
Employees interested in professional social media presence can join the Perficient Voices program:
- Training on personal branding and content creation
- Content suggestions and calendar aligned with company themes
- Marketing support for amplifying high-quality posts
- LinkedIn profile optimization guidance
- Monthly analytics on reach and engagement

### 4.2 Blogging
Employees are encouraged to write for Perficient's blog:
- Submit topics to the Content team (content@perficient.com)
- First draft due within 2 weeks of topic approval
- Editorial review and SEO optimization by Marketing
- Published under employee's byline with Perficient branding
- Cross-promoted on company social channels

### 4.3 External Publications
Writing for external publications (Medium, DZone, industry blogs):
- Must not include client confidential information
- Must include Perficient affiliation in author bio
- Should not duplicate content published on Perficient's blog
- Technical content should be reviewed for accuracy by a peer
- Notify Marketing for coordination and amplification

---

## 5. Client-Related Social Media

### 5.1 Client Project Content
- Never post about client projects without explicit written permission
- No photos of client offices, systems, or team members without consent
- Do not tag clients in posts without their approval
- Case studies require client legal approval before publication

### 5.2 Client References
- Social endorsements of clients are permitted if they are factual and positive
- Do not publicly discuss client challenges, issues, or internal dynamics
- Award submissions mentioning client work require client approval

---

## 6. Crisis Communications on Social Media

### 6.1 During Company Crisis
If Perficient is experiencing a public crisis:
- Do not comment on the situation on personal social media
- Do not respond to media inquiries via social DMs
- Direct all inquiries to media@perficient.com
- Wait for official company statement before engaging
- Follow Marketing's specific guidance for the situation

### 6.2 Misinformation
If you see inaccurate information about Perficient circulating:
- Do not engage directly or argue publicly
- Notify Marketing immediately (socialmedia@perficient.com)
- Marketing will coordinate appropriate response

---

## 7. Platform-Specific Guidelines

### 7.1 LinkedIn
- Keep profile current with accurate Perficient title and description
- Engage professionally with connection requests and messages
- Share relevant company and industry content regularly
- Do not mass-connect or use automation tools for engagement

### 7.2 GitHub and Technical Platforms
- Clearly distinguish personal projects from Perficient work
- Do not commit client code or proprietary Perficient code to personal repos
- Open source contributions on company time require manager approval
- Use appropriate licenses (consult Legal if unsure)

### 7.3 Stack Overflow and Technical Forums
- May answer questions related to general technology expertise
- Do not share proprietary solutions or client-specific implementations
- Represent yourself accurately (current role and experience)

---

## 8. Monitoring and Enforcement

### 8.1 Social Media Monitoring
Marketing monitors mentions of Perficient across social platforms for:
- Brand sentiment and reputation
- Potential policy violations
- Crisis early warning
- Competitive intelligence

### 8.2 Violations
Policy violations are addressed through:
- First offense: Coaching conversation with manager and Marketing
- Second offense: Written warning, mandatory social media training
- Serious violation (data breach, client harm): Immediate HR investigation, potential termination
- Content removal: Employee must remove violating content immediately upon request
