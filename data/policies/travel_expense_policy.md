# Perficient Travel and Expense Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Last Updated:** February 28, 2024  
**Policy Owner:** Chief Financial Officer  
**Applies To:** All Perficient employees authorized to travel on company business

---

## 1. General Principles

Perficient reimburses reasonable and necessary business expenses incurred while conducting company business. Employees are expected to exercise good judgment and spend company funds as they would their own money. All expenses must have a legitimate business purpose.

---

## 2. Travel Authorization

### 2.1 Pre-Approval Requirements
- **Domestic travel (within US):** Requires manager approval for trips exceeding $500 total estimated cost
- **International travel:** Requires VP-level approval minimum 2 weeks in advance
- **Client-site travel:** Must be pre-approved by the project manager and documented in the SOW

### 2.2 Booking Guidelines
All travel must be booked through Perficient's approved travel management platform (Concur Travel). Benefits of using Concur:
- Negotiated corporate rates
- Automatic policy compliance checking
- Integrated expense reporting
- 24/7 travel support

Booking outside of Concur is permitted only when Concur rates are more than 20% higher than publicly available rates (screenshot required for documentation).

---

## 3. Air Travel

### 3.1 Class of Service
- **Domestic flights (under 5 hours):** Economy class
- **International flights (5+ hours):** Premium Economy; Business class requires VP approval
- **Flights over 8 hours:** Business class with VP approval
- **Directors and above:** Business class for flights over 4 hours

### 3.2 Booking Advance
Flights should be booked at least 14 days in advance when possible to secure lower fares. Last-minute bookings (within 7 days) require manager justification.

### 3.3 Airline Loyalty Programs
Employees may retain personal frequent flyer miles earned on business travel. However, flight selections must prioritize cost and schedule over loyalty program preference.

### 3.4 Baggage
One checked bag is reimbursable for trips of 3+ nights. Carry-on/personal item bag fees are reimbursable for all trips.

---

## 4. Lodging

### 4.1 Hotel Guidelines
- Book hotels through Concur to access corporate rates
- Maximum nightly rate: $200/night for domestic travel (higher-cost cities like NYC, SF, DC: $300/night)
- Extended stay (5+ nights): Consider corporate apartment or extended-stay hotel
- Room type: Standard room (suites require VP approval)

### 4.2 Preferred Hotels
Perficient has negotiated rates with Marriott, Hilton, and Hyatt properties. Use preferred hotels when available within policy limits.

### 4.3 Alternative Accommodations
Airbnb and similar services are permitted when:
- Cost is equal to or less than comparable hotel options
- The property has a minimum 4.5-star rating
- Booked through Concur's vacation rental integration

---

## 5. Ground Transportation

### 5.1 Rental Cars
- Compact or intermediate class for individual travelers
- Full-size permitted for groups of 3+ employees
- Fuel: Company reimburses fuel for business use
- Always accept the rental company's insurance waiver (Perficient's corporate insurance covers rentals booked through Concur)
- Return with a full tank to avoid refueling surcharges

### 5.2 Ride-Share Services
Uber and Lyft are approved for business travel. Use standard (UberX/Lyft) service; premium services (Uber Black, Lyft Lux) are not reimbursable without VP approval.

### 5.3 Personal Vehicle
Mileage reimbursement for business use of personal vehicles: $0.67/mile (2024 IRS rate). Commuting mileage (home to office) is not reimbursable. Document starting/ending odometer readings.

### 5.4 Parking and Tolls
Reasonable parking and toll expenses are reimbursable with receipts. Airport parking: economy/remote lot rates only unless trip is under 3 days.

---

## 6. Meals and Entertainment

### 6.1 Per Diem Rates (Daily Meal Allowance)
Instead of itemized receipts, employees may claim the daily meal per diem:
- **Domestic travel:** $75/day
- **High-cost cities (NYC, SF, DC, Boston, Chicago):** $100/day
- **International travel:** Based on U.S. State Department rates for the destination country

### 6.2 Individual Meal Limits (if not using per diem)
- Breakfast: $20
- Lunch: $25
- Dinner: $50
- Incidentals (snacks, coffee): $10/day

### 6.3 Client Entertainment
Client meals and entertainment require:
- Pre-approval from project manager
- Documentation of attendees and business purpose
- Reasonable limits: $100/person for dinner, $50/person for lunch
- Alcohol is reimbursable in moderation at client dinners (max 2 drinks per person)

### 6.4 Team Meals
Managers may expense team meals for:
- Project celebrations and milestones
- Team building events (quarterly, max $50/person)
- Working lunches during all-day meetings

---

## 7. Expense Reporting

### 7.1 Submission Deadlines
- Expense reports must be submitted within 14 days of trip completion
- Monthly recurring expenses (internet, phone) are due by the 5th of the following month
- Reports submitted more than 60 days late may not be reimbursed

### 7.2 Required Documentation
All expenses over $25 require an itemized receipt. The following information must be included:
- Date of expense
- Vendor/merchant name
- Itemized charges (not just a total)
- Business purpose
- Attendees (for meals with others)

### 7.3 Approval Workflow
1. Employee submits expense report in Concur
2. Direct manager reviews and approves (within 5 business days)
3. Finance team processes approved reports
4. Reimbursement via direct deposit within 10 business days of final approval

### 7.4 Corporate Credit Card
Employees who travel frequently may be issued a Perficient corporate credit card (Chase Visa). Guidelines:
- Used only for business expenses
- Personal charges are prohibited
- Statements must be reconciled monthly in Concur
- Lost/stolen cards: Call Chase immediately and notify Finance

---

## 8. Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following expenses are NOT reimbursable:
- Personal entertainment (movies, sightseeing, spa services)
- Traffic violations and parking tickets
- Pet care/kennel fees
- Personal travel insurance
- First-class upgrades (unless pre-approved)
- Mini-bar charges
- Laundry/dry cleaning (unless trip exceeds 5 nights)
- Personal phone calls exceeding $10/day
- Expenses for travel companions (spouses, family members)
- Gift cards or cash equivalents

---

## 9. International Travel

### 9.1 Additional Requirements
- Passport must be valid for 6+ months beyond travel dates
- Visa costs are reimbursable
- Travel medical insurance: Provided through Perficient's corporate plan
- Vaccinations: Reimbursable when required for destination

### 9.2 Foreign Currency
- Use the corporate credit card for foreign transactions (no foreign transaction fees)
- Currency exchange fees are reimbursable
- Report expenses in USD using the exchange rate on the date of transaction

### 9.3 Safety
- Register travel with Global Security through Concur
- Download the International SOS app for emergency assistance
- Avoid traveling to regions with State Department Level 3 or 4 advisories without CISO and VP approval

---

## 10. Work-From-Home Expenses

### 10.1 Internet Reimbursement
Remote employees receive a $75/month internet stipend. No receipt required; claimed as a monthly expense in Concur.

### 10.2 Home Office Equipment
New hires receive a one-time $500 home office setup stipend for ergonomic equipment (desk, chair, monitor). Receipts required.

### 10.3 Office Supplies
Reasonable office supplies (notebooks, pens, printer paper) are reimbursable up to $50/quarter without pre-approval.
