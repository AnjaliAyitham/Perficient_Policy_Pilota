# Perficient Acceptable Use Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Last Updated:** February 15, 2024  
**Policy Owner:** Chief Information Officer  
**Applies To:** All users of Perficient technology resources (employees, contractors, guests)

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the acceptable use of Perficient's technology resources including computers, networks, software, email, internet access, and cloud services. Proper use protects Perficient, its employees, and its clients from security risks and legal liability.

---

## 2. Ownership and Monitoring

### 2.1 Company Property
All technology resources provided by Perficient (laptops, phones, software licenses, accounts) remain company property. Perficient reserves the right to:
- Monitor usage of company resources
- Access data stored on company systems
- Revoke access at any time for business or security reasons
- Inspect devices during security investigations

### 2.2 No Expectation of Privacy
Employees should have no expectation of privacy when using company technology resources. This includes email, internet browsing, chat messages, file storage, and phone calls made on company devices.

### 2.3 Monitoring Practices
Perficient employs automated monitoring for:
- Data Loss Prevention (DLP): Detecting sensitive data leaving the organization
- Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR): Identifying security threats
- Network traffic analysis: Detecting unusual patterns
- Email filtering: Blocking phishing and malware

---

## 3. Permitted Use

### 3.1 Business Use
Company technology resources are provided primarily for conducting Perficient business. This includes:
- Client project work and deliverables
- Internal communication and collaboration
- Research and professional development
- Business travel planning and expense management
- Accessing company applications and services

### 3.2 Limited Personal Use
Reasonable personal use is permitted provided it:
- Does not interfere with job performance
- Does not consume excessive bandwidth or storage
- Does not violate any other company policy
- Does not create legal liability for Perficient
- Occurs during breaks or outside of core working hours when possible

Examples of acceptable personal use:
- Checking personal email during breaks
- Brief personal calls
- Online banking
- News and social media browsing (non-excessive)
- Streaming music at reasonable volume (with headphones)

---

## 4. Prohibited Activities

### 4.1 Strictly Prohibited
The following activities are strictly prohibited on company resources regardless of circumstances:

**Security Violations:**
- Attempting to bypass security controls or access unauthorized systems
- Sharing passwords or authentication credentials
- Installing unauthorized software or tools
- Running network scanning or hacking tools without Security approval
- Connecting unauthorized devices to the corporate network
- Disabling or tampering with security software

**Illegal Activities:**
- Downloading, distributing, or storing pirated software, music, movies, or other copyrighted content
- Accessing, possessing, or distributing illegal material
- Engaging in fraud, theft, or other criminal activity
- Harassing, threatening, or discriminating against others
- Gambling (in jurisdictions where prohibited)

**Harmful Activities:**
- Cryptocurrency mining on company hardware
- Running personal businesses on company equipment
- Excessive bandwidth consumption (torrenting, large personal downloads)
- Sending spam or bulk unsolicited emails
- Posting company confidential information externally
- Creating or distributing malware

**Inappropriate Content:**
- Accessing pornographic or sexually explicit material
- Downloading or displaying offensive, violent, or hateful content
- Sending or forwarding offensive jokes, images, or messages

### 4.2 Activities Requiring Approval
The following require explicit written approval from IT Security:
- Using USB storage devices for data transfer
- Installing development tools not in the Software Center
- Accessing the dark web (for research purposes only)
- Running penetration testing tools
- Setting up personal cloud storage integrations
- Using VPN services other than GlobalProtect

---

## 5. Email Use

### 5.1 Professional Standards
Email communications should be:
- Professional in tone and content
- Clear and concise
- Properly addressed (verify recipients before sending sensitive content)
- Free of large unnecessary attachments (use SharePoint links instead)

### 5.2 Email Restrictions
- Do not send Restricted or Confidential data via unencrypted email
- Do not auto-forward company email to personal accounts
- Do not use personal email for business communications
- Do not open attachments from unknown or suspicious senders
- Maximum attachment size: 25 MB (use OneDrive/SharePoint for larger files)

### 5.3 Email Retention
- Business emails: Retained per records management policy (7 years default)
- Deletion: Do not delete emails that may be subject to legal hold
- Personal emails on company systems: No retention guarantee

---

## 6. Internet and Web Use

### 6.1 Web Filtering
Perficient employs web content filtering that blocks:
- Known malicious websites
- Phishing and scam sites
- Pornographic content
- Gambling sites
- Unauthorized file-sharing platforms

### 6.2 Cloud Services
Only use approved cloud services for business data:
- **Approved:** Microsoft 365, SharePoint, OneDrive for Business, Teams, approved Azure/AWS instances
- **Prohibited for business data:** Personal Google Drive, Dropbox, WeTransfer, personal iCloud

### 6.3 Downloads
- Only download software from approved sources (Software Center, official vendor sites)
- Verify file integrity when downloading development tools
- Report any unexpected downloads or pop-ups to IT Security

---

## 7. Mobile Device Use

### 7.1 Company-Issued Devices
- Must be protected with biometric lock or 6+ digit PIN
- Must remain updated with latest OS and security patches
- Must not be jailbroken or rooted
- Report loss or theft immediately to IT Service Desk

### 7.2 Personal Devices (BYOD)
If enrolled in company MDM:
- Company may remotely wipe the device in case of loss/theft
- Company data is containerized (personal apps/data not accessed)
- Must meet minimum security requirements
- Employee may unenroll at any time (company data will be removed)

---

## 8. Software and Licensing

### 8.1 Licensed Software
- Only use software that Perficient has properly licensed
- Do not install personal software licenses on company equipment
- Do not share license keys between users
- Report any BSA/SIIA audit requests to Legal immediately

### 8.2 Open Source
Open source software usage in client projects must:
- Comply with the open source license terms
- Be reviewed by the Architecture team for license compatibility
- Be documented in the project's software bill of materials (SBOM)
- Not introduce copyleft obligations without Legal approval

### 8.3 AI Tools
Use of AI coding assistants and large language models:
- Approved: GitHub Copilot (company license), Claude Code (via Portkey)
- Prohibited: Pasting client code or confidential data into public AI tools
- All AI-generated code must be reviewed for security and license compliance
- Do not use AI tools to process PII or sensitive client data

---

## 9. Enforcement

### 9.1 Monitoring and Detection
IT Security monitors for policy violations through:
- Automated DLP alerts
- Web proxy logs
- Email content scanning
- Endpoint behavior analysis
- Periodic audits

### 9.2 Consequences
Violations of this policy may result in:
- Warning and mandatory security training (first minor offense)
- Written disciplinary action (repeated minor or first major offense)
- Suspension of technology access (serious violations)
- Termination of employment (severe or repeated violations)
- Legal action (illegal activities, data theft)

### 9.3 Reporting Violations
Report suspected violations to:
- IT Security: security@perficient.com
- Ethics Hotline: 1-800-PRFT-ETH (anonymous)
- Direct manager or HR Business Partner
