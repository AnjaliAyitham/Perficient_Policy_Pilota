# Perficient Intellectual Property Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Last Updated:** February 5, 2024  
**Policy Owner:** General Counsel  
**Applies To:** All Perficient employees and contractors who create work product

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines ownership, protection, and use of intellectual property (IP) at Perficient. Clear IP guidelines protect the company's competitive advantages, ensure proper client deliverables, and respect employees' creative contributions.

---

## 2. Company-Owned Intellectual Property

### 2.1 Work Product Assignment
All work product created by employees during employment and within the scope of their duties is the exclusive property of Perficient. This includes:
- Source code, software, and applications
- Documentation, designs, and specifications
- Processes, methodologies, and frameworks
- Inventions, discoveries, and improvements
- Training materials and presentations
- Research and analysis

### 2.2 Scope of Assignment
Work product is company-owned when created:
- During working hours
- Using company resources (hardware, software, data, facilities)
- Related to Perficient's current or anticipated business
- As part of job responsibilities or assigned tasks
- Under client engagement contracts

### 2.3 Assignment Agreement
All employees sign an Intellectual Property Assignment Agreement upon hire. This agreement:
- Assigns all qualifying work product to Perficient
- Requires disclosure of inventions conceived during employment
- Survives termination for work created during employment
- Includes state-specific exceptions (e.g., California Labor Code §2870)

---

## 3. Client Intellectual Property

### 3.1 Client Data and Systems
When working on client engagements:
- Client existing IP remains client property at all times
- Access client IP only as needed for authorized work
- Never copy, retain, or reuse client IP on other engagements
- Return or destroy all client IP at engagement completion
- Respect client's proprietary tools and methodologies

### 3.2 Custom Deliverables
IP ownership for custom client deliverables is governed by the SOW/MSA:
- **Default:** Client owns custom work product (most common)
- **License model:** Perficient retains ownership, grants client perpetual license
- **Joint ownership:** Rare, requires specific contract language

### 3.3 Background IP
Perficient's pre-existing IP (accelerators, frameworks, utilities) used in client projects:
- Remains Perficient property
- Client receives non-exclusive license to use in context of delivered solution
- Must be identified in the SOW before use in client project
- Cannot be modified to become client-exclusive without Legal approval

## 4. Open Source

### 4.1 Using Open Source
When incorporating open source software:
- Verify license compatibility with project requirements
- Document all open source dependencies in SBOM (Software Bill of Materials)
- Follow license obligations (attribution, source availability for copyleft)
- Obtain Architecture review for copyleft licenses (GPL, AGPL) in client deliverables
- Never include open source code without understanding its license

### 4.2 Open Source License Categories

| Category | Risk Level | Examples | Usage |
|----------|-----------|----------|-------|
| Permissive | Low | MIT, BSD, Apache 2.0 | Generally acceptable in all projects |
| Weak Copyleft | Medium | LGPL, MPL | Acceptable with proper isolation |
| Strong Copyleft | High | GPL, AGPL | Requires Legal review; usually not in commercial deliverables |
| Commercial | Varies | Proprietary licenses | Must be properly licensed |

### 4.3 Contributing to Open Source
Employees wishing to contribute to open source projects:
- Personal time, personal code: No approval needed (ensure no Perficient IP)
- Company time, company-related code: Requires manager and Legal approval
- Opening new Perficient-owned projects: Requires CTO and Legal approval
- Contributions must not expose proprietary algorithms or client information

## 5. Inventions and Patents

### 5.1 Invention Disclosure
Employees who develop novel inventions, processes, or improvements must:
1. Submit an Invention Disclosure Form to Legal within 30 days
2. Include description, potential applications, and development timeline
3. Identify all contributors
4. Do not publicly disclose before patent review

### 5.2 Patent Evaluation
Legal evaluates disclosures for:
- Novelty and patentability
- Business value and strategic importance
- Estimated cost vs. benefit of patent filing
- Prior art research

### 5.3 Inventor Recognition
Employees named as inventors on patent applications:
- Receive $2,000 bonus upon patent filing
- Receive additional $3,000 upon patent grant
- Recognized in company communications
- Patent contributions considered in performance reviews

## 6. Personal Projects

### 6.1 Permitted Personal Projects
Employees may work on personal projects outside of work provided:
- Created entirely on personal time
- Using personal equipment and resources
- Not related to Perficient's current business or client work
- Not competing with Perficient's services
- Not using skills or knowledge gained solely from confidential Perficient information

### 6.2 Disclosure Requirement
Employees should disclose personal projects to their manager when:
- The project is in a technology area where Perficient operates
- There is potential perception of conflict of interest
- The project may be commercialized
- The employee is uncertain about whether the project overlaps with Perficient's interests

### 6.3 Moonlighting
Outside employment or consulting:
- Must not conflict with Perficient duties or create conflicts of interest
- Must not involve Perficient clients without written approval
- Requires disclosure via the Conflict of Interest form
- Must not use Perficient time, equipment, or proprietary information

## 7. Trademarks and Branding

### 7.1 Perficient Brand Assets
- Perficient name, logos, and taglines are registered trademarks
- Only use official brand assets from the Brand Portal
- Do not modify logos, change colors, or create derivative marks
- Use ™ or ® symbols as directed in Brand Guidelines

### 7.2 Third-Party Trademarks
- Respect others' trademarks (use proper capitalization, attribution)
- Do not use partner/client logos without written permission
- Include appropriate trademark notices in marketing materials
- Follow technology partners' brand guidelines (Microsoft, Adobe, Salesforce)

---

## 8. Confidential Information Protection

### 8.1 Trade Secrets
Perficient trade secrets include:
- Proprietary development methodologies and accelerators
- Client lists and relationship details
- Pricing models and competitive intelligence
- Internal financial data and forecasts
- Strategic plans and roadmaps
- Recruitment and compensation data

### 8.2 Protection Measures
- Access restricted to need-to-know basis
- Classified as "Confidential" or "Restricted" per data classification policy
- Protected by NDA with all employees and contractors
- Cannot be disclosed during or after employment
- Legal action pursued for unauthorized disclosure

### 8.3 Post-Employment Obligations
Upon leaving Perficient:
- Return all company materials and IP
- Continue to protect trade secrets and confidential information indefinitely
- Non-compete provisions apply per employment agreement (where enforceable)
- Non-solicitation of employees and clients per agreement terms (typically 12 months)
