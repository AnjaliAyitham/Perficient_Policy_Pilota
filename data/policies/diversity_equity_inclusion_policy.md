# Perficient Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI) Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Last Updated:** January 15, 2024  
**Policy Owner:** Chief People Officer & VP of DEI  
**Applies To:** All Perficient employees, contractors, and recruiting processes

---

## 1. Our Commitment

Perficient is committed to building a diverse, equitable, and inclusive workplace where all colleagues feel they belong, are valued, and can do their best work. We believe diverse perspectives drive better solutions for our clients and stronger business outcomes.

---

## 2. Equal Employment Opportunity

### 2.1 Non-Discrimination
Perficient provides equal employment opportunities regardless of:
- Race, color, ethnicity, or national origin
- Gender, gender identity, or gender expression
- Sexual orientation
- Age
- Disability (physical or mental)
- Religion or creed
- Veteran or military status
- Pregnancy or parental status
- Genetic information
- Marital or familial status
- Any other protected characteristic under federal, state, or local law

### 2.2 Application to Employment Decisions
This commitment applies to all employment decisions including:
- Hiring and recruiting
- Compensation and benefits
- Promotion and advancement
- Training and development
- Work assignments and transfers
- Disciplinary action and termination
- Layoffs and recalls

---

## 3. Inclusive Hiring Practices

### 3.1 Diverse Candidate Slates
- All open positions must have a diverse candidate slate before interviews begin
- "Diverse" means at least one candidate from an underrepresented group in the final interview round
- Recruiters are trained on sourcing from diverse talent pools
- Job descriptions are reviewed for inclusive language (no gendered terms, unnecessary requirements)

### 3.2 Interview Practices
- Structured interviews with standardized questions for all candidates
- Diverse interview panels (minimum 1 interviewer from an underrepresented group)
- Unconscious bias training required for all interviewers (refreshed annually)
- Skills-based assessments over credential-based filtering where possible
- Blind resume reviews for initial screening when feasible

### 3.3 Partnerships
Perficient partners with organizations that promote diversity in tech:
- Historically Black Colleges and Universities (HBCUs)
- Women in Technology organizations
- Veterans employment programs
- Disability employment networks
- LGBTQ+ professional organizations

---

## 4. Pay Equity

### 4.1 Commitment
Perficient is committed to fair and equitable compensation regardless of gender, race, or other protected characteristics.

### 4.2 Pay Equity Analysis
- Annual pay equity audit conducted by independent third party
- Analysis covers base pay, total compensation, and promotion rates
- Results shared with Executive Leadership and Board
- Identified disparities are remediated within one compensation cycle

### 4.3 Transparency
- Salary ranges published for all job postings (compliant with state pay transparency laws)
- Employees may discuss compensation without fear of retaliation
- Compensation philosophy documented and shared with all employees

## 5. Employee Resource Groups (ERGs)

### 5.1 Active ERGs
Perficient supports the following Employee Resource Groups:
- **PRFT Women:** Supporting women's advancement and development
- **PRFT Pride:** LGBTQ+ community and allies
- **PRFT Black Professionals Network:** Black employee community and development
- **PRFT LatinX:** Hispanic and Latino community
- **PRFT Veterans:** Military veterans and their families
- **PRFT Asian Alliance:** Asian and Pacific Islander community
- **PRFT Abilities:** Employees with disabilities and caregivers
- **PRFT Next Gen:** Early career professionals

### 5.2 ERG Support
- Each ERG receives $10,000 annual budget for programming
- Executive sponsor assigned to each ERG
- ERG leaders receive 4 hours/month of paid time for ERG activities
- ERG membership is open to all employees (members and allies)
- ERGs advise on company policies, benefits, and cultural initiatives

### 5.3 Starting a New ERG
Employees interested in forming a new ERG should:
1. Gather a minimum of 15 interested members
2. Identify a leadership team (Chair, Vice-Chair, Treasurer)
3. Submit a charter to the DEI team
4. Present to the Executive Sponsor committee
5. Receive approval and onboarding support

## 6. Accessibility

### 6.1 Workplace Accommodations
Perficient provides reasonable accommodations for employees with disabilities:
- Modified work schedules or telecommuting arrangements
- Assistive technology and adaptive equipment
- Accessible workspaces and facilities
- Sign language interpreters or captioning services
- Modified job duties when essential functions can still be performed

### 6.2 Requesting Accommodations
1. Contact HR Business Partner or accommodations@perficient.com
2. Engage in interactive process to determine effective accommodation
3. Medical documentation may be requested (kept confidential)
4. Accommodation implemented within 10 business days of approval
5. Periodic review to ensure accommodation remains effective

### 6.3 Digital Accessibility
- All internal applications must meet WCAG 2.1 AA standards
- Client deliverables should meet accessibility standards per SOW
- Accessibility testing included in QA processes
- Training available for developers on accessible design

## 7. Inclusive Culture Practices

### 7.1 Meetings
- Distribute agendas in advance so all participants can prepare
- Actively invite quieter voices to contribute
- Provide virtual participation options for remote employees
- Record meetings for those in different time zones
- Use inclusive language (avoid jargon, idioms that don't translate)

### 7.2 Communication
- Use gender-neutral language in policies and communications
- Respect chosen names and pronouns (include pronouns in email signatures)
- Provide materials in accessible formats upon request
- Offer translation services for non-native English speakers when needed

### 7.3 Cultural Awareness
- Acknowledge and respect diverse cultural practices and holidays
- Avoid scheduling critical meetings on major religious or cultural holidays
- Support employees observing religious practices (prayer times, dietary needs)
- Celebrate cultural heritage months with programming and education

## 8. Anti-Harassment and Anti-Discrimination

### 8.1 Zero Tolerance
Perficient maintains zero tolerance for harassment and discrimination. Prohibited behaviors include:
- Racial slurs, jokes, or stereotyping
- Sexual harassment (verbal, physical, visual, digital)
- Bullying or intimidation
- Microaggressions (subtle, often unintentional discriminatory comments)
- Exclusion from meetings, decisions, or social activities based on protected characteristics
- Retaliating against those who report concerns

### 8.2 Reporting
Report incidents through:
- Direct manager (if not the subject of complaint)
- HR Business Partner
- DEI team: dei@perficient.com
- Ethics Hotline: 1-800-PRFT-ETH (anonymous)
- Any member of leadership

### 8.3 Investigation and Resolution
- All reports investigated within 5 business days
- Confidentiality maintained to the extent possible
- Both parties informed of outcome
- Remedial action taken where violation confirmed
- Follow-up check with reporting party at 30 and 90 days

## 9. Training and Development

### 9.1 Required Training
- **All employees:** Unconscious bias awareness (annual)
- **All employees:** Inclusive workplace training (annual)
- **Managers:** Inclusive leadership (semi-annual)
- **Recruiters:** Equitable hiring practices (semi-annual)
- **New hires:** DEI orientation module (during onboarding)

### 9.2 Development Programs
- Sponsorship program for underrepresented employees (VP-nominated)
- Mentoring circles (cross-functional, identity-based available)
- Leadership development cohorts with diversity goals
- Conference sponsorships for ERG members

---

## 10. Metrics and Accountability

### 10.1 DEI Metrics Tracked
- Workforce demographics (by level, function, geography)
- Hiring pipeline diversity (applicant → interview → offer → accept)
- Promotion rates by demographic group
- Attrition rates by demographic group
- Pay equity ratios
- Employee engagement survey results (inclusion index)
- ERG participation rates

### 10.2 Reporting
- Quarterly DEI dashboard shared with Executive Leadership
- Annual DEI report published company-wide
- Board receives DEI update semi-annually
- Progress against goals reviewed in leadership quarterly business reviews

### 10.3 Manager Accountability
- DEI goals included in manager performance evaluations (10% weighting)
- Managers assessed on team diversity, inclusive behaviors, and development of underrepresented talent
- Recognition for managers who demonstrate inclusive leadership
