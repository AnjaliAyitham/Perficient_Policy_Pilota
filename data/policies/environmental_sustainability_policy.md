# Perficient Environmental Sustainability Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Last Updated:** February 28, 2024  
**Policy Owner:** VP of Corporate Social Responsibility  
**Applies To:** All Perficient operations, employees, and business decisions

---

## 1. Commitment

Perficient is committed to reducing our environmental impact and contributing to a sustainable future. As a digital consultancy, our primary environmental footprint comes from energy consumption (offices and data centers), business travel, and electronic waste. We are committed to achieving carbon neutrality in our operations by 2027.

---

## 2. Carbon Reduction Goals

### 2.1 Targets
- **2025:** 40% reduction in Scope 1 and 2 emissions (vs. 2020 baseline)
- **2027:** Carbon neutral operations (Scope 1 and 2)
- **2030:** 50% reduction in Scope 3 emissions (business travel, supply chain)
- **2035:** Net-zero across all scopes

### 2.2 Scope Definitions
- **Scope 1:** Direct emissions (company vehicles, on-site fuel combustion)
- **Scope 2:** Indirect emissions from purchased electricity, heating, cooling
- **Scope 3:** All other indirect emissions (business travel, employee commuting, purchased goods/services, data center usage)

---

## 3. Office Operations

### 3.1 Energy Efficiency
- LED lighting in all offices (completed 2023)
- Smart HVAC systems with occupancy sensors
- Power management settings on all company devices
- Renewable energy procurement where available (RECs for remaining)
- ENERGY STAR rated appliances and equipment

### 3.2 Waste Reduction
- Paperless operations: Default to digital (printing requires justification)
- Recycling stations on every floor (paper, plastic, metal, glass)
- Composting available in cafeteria locations
- Single-use plastic eliminated from break rooms (reusable dishware provided)
- E-waste recycled through certified R2 recycler

### 3.3 Water Conservation
- Low-flow fixtures in all restrooms
- Sensor-operated faucets and toilets
- Landscaping with native, drought-resistant plants
- No bottled water — filtered water stations provided

### 3.4 Green Building
- LEED certification pursued for new office leases
- Energy audits conducted annually for all office locations
- Green cleaning products used exclusively
- Indoor plants for air quality and employee wellbeing

---

## 4. Business Travel

### 4.1 Travel Reduction
- Virtual-first approach for internal meetings (travel for client-facing meetings primarily)
- Consolidated trips when visiting same region
- Manager justification required for domestic travel (could this be a video call?)
- International travel requires clear business necessity beyond VP approval

### 4.2 Sustainable Travel Choices
- Train preferred over air for trips under 300 miles
- Direct flights preferred (reduce emissions from takeoff/landing)
- Fuel-efficient or electric rental cars when available
- Carbon offset purchased for all air travel (company-funded, automatic through Concur)
- Hotels with sustainability certifications preferred

### 4.3 Commuting
- Remote work policy reduces daily commuting
- Public transit subsidy: Up to $300/month pre-tax
- Bike-to-work program: $50/month stipend, secure bike parking, shower facilities
- Electric vehicle charging stations at office locations (where available)
- Carpool matching program through internal platform

---

## 5. Technology and Data Centers

### 5.1 Cloud Efficiency
- Prioritize cloud regions powered by renewable energy
- Right-size cloud resources (avoid over-provisioning)
- Auto-scaling and shutdown of non-production environments outside business hours
- Annual cloud sustainability review with Azure/AWS account teams

### 5.2 Hardware Lifecycle
- Laptop refresh cycle: 4 years (extended from 3 years to reduce e-waste)
- Retired devices: Wiped and donated to nonprofits when functional
- Non-functional devices: Recycled through certified e-waste partners
- Packaging: Prefer minimal, recyclable packaging from vendors

### 5.3 Sustainable Software Practices
- Efficient code reduces compute requirements (green software principles)
- Optimize CI/CD pipelines to reduce unnecessary builds
- Archive or decommission unused applications and data
- Consider carbon impact in architecture decisions (serverless vs. always-on)

---

## 6. Supply Chain

### 6.1 Vendor Sustainability Criteria
When selecting vendors, consider:
- Vendor's public sustainability commitments and reporting
- Environmental certifications (ISO 14001, B Corp)
- Carbon footprint of their products/services
- Sustainable packaging and shipping practices
- Local sourcing preference to reduce transportation emissions

### 6.2 Procurement Guidelines
- Office supplies: Recycled content preferred (minimum 30% post-consumer)
- Furniture: FSC-certified wood, recycled materials
- Catering: Local, seasonal, plant-based options available
- Promotional items: Sustainable materials only (no single-use plastic giveaways)

---

## 7. Employee Engagement

### 7.1 Green Team
Employee-led Green Team coordinates sustainability initiatives:
- Monthly awareness campaigns
- Quarterly volunteer events (park cleanups, tree planting)
- Annual Earth Week programming
- Suggestion program for sustainability improvements
- Budget: $15,000/year from CSR budget

### 7.2 Personal Sustainability Support
- Free home energy audit (one-time benefit for homeowners)
- EV purchase discount through corporate partnerships
- Community solar program information
- Sustainability education resources on Perficient Academy

### 7.3 Recognition
- Annual "Green Champion" award for outstanding sustainability contributions
- Department sustainability scorecards
- Sustainability metrics included in corporate social responsibility report

---

## 8. Reporting and Transparency

### 8.1 Annual Sustainability Report
Published annually (Q2) covering:
- Greenhouse gas emissions inventory (Scope 1, 2, and 3)
- Progress against reduction targets
- Energy consumption and renewable energy percentage
- Waste diversion rate
- Business travel carbon footprint
- Water usage
- Key initiatives and achievements

### 8.2 Standards and Frameworks
Reporting aligned with:
- GHG Protocol (emissions accounting)
- TCFD (climate-related financial disclosures)
- CDP (environmental disclosure)
- UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs 7, 12, 13)

### 8.3 Third-Party Verification
- Annual emissions inventory verified by independent auditor
- Renewable energy claims verified through REC tracking
- Carbon offset quality verified (Gold Standard or Verified Carbon Standard)

---

## 9. Compliance and Accountability

### 9.1 Executive Accountability
- CSR goals included in executive performance metrics
- Board receives sustainability update quarterly
- CFO oversees carbon accounting and reporting
- VP of Facilities owns operational sustainability targets

### 9.2 Employee Responsibility
All employees contribute by:
- Turning off equipment when not in use
- Minimizing printing and paper usage
- Choosing sustainable travel options when available
- Properly sorting waste and recycling
- Reporting sustainability improvement ideas
- Participating in Green Team initiatives

### 9.3 Policy Review
- Annual review and update of sustainability targets
- Triggered updates for: New regulations, material changes in operations, significant goal progress or setbacks
- Stakeholder input gathered through employee survey and Green Team
