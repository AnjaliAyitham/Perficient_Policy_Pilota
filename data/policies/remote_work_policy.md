# Perficient Remote Work Policy

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Last Updated:** March 1, 2024  
**Policy Owner:** Chief People Officer  
**Applies To:** All Perficient employees eligible for remote or hybrid work arrangements

---

## 1. Work Arrangement Types

### 1.1 Fully Remote
Employees who work from home or a location of their choice 100% of the time. No requirement to report to a Perficient office. Must reside within an approved state/country for tax and employment law purposes.

### 1.2 Hybrid
Employees who split time between a Perficient office and remote work. Standard hybrid expectation: 2-3 days per week in office, as determined by team and manager.

### 1.3 Office-Based
Employees who work from a Perficient office 4-5 days per week. Typically roles requiring physical presence (facilities, on-site client work, hardware labs).

---

## 2. Eligibility

### 2.1 Remote Work Eligibility Criteria
Remote work is available to employees who:
- Have completed their 90-day probationary period
- Maintain a "Meets Expectations" or higher performance rating
- Are in a role that can be performed effectively without physical office presence
- Have manager and HR approval for their work arrangement

### 2.2 Roles Typically Eligible
- Software Engineers and Architects
- Project Managers and Scrum Masters
- Business Analysts
- Data Engineers and Scientists
- UX/UI Designers
- Technical Consultants
- Sales and Account Managers (with client travel as needed)

### 2.3 Roles Typically Office-Based
- IT Infrastructure/Network Engineers (hardware-dependent)
- Facilities and Office Management
- Roles with specific client on-site requirements
- New hires during onboarding period (first 30 days)

---

## 3. Home Office Requirements

### 3.1 Workspace Standards
Remote employees must maintain a dedicated workspace that:
- Provides a quiet, professional environment for video calls
- Has reliable high-speed internet (minimum 50 Mbps download, 10 Mbps upload)
- Is ergonomically appropriate (proper desk, chair, monitor height)
- Is free from regular interruptions during working hours
- Has adequate lighting for video conferencing

### 3.2 Equipment Provided by Perficient
- Laptop computer (refreshed every 3 years)
- External monitor (one 27" monitor for standard roles; dual monitors for developers)
- Keyboard and mouse
- Headset with microphone
- Laptop stand or docking station

### 3.3 Employee-Provided
- Internet service (reimbursed at $75/month)
- Desk and chair (one-time $500 stipend for new remote employees)
- Appropriate physical workspace
- Backup internet solution recommended (mobile hotspot)

---

## 4. Working Hours and Availability

### 4.1 Core Collaboration Hours
All employees regardless of location must be available during core hours: **10:00 AM - 3:00 PM** in their local time zone. Outside core hours, employees may flex their schedule with manager approval.

### 4.2 Time Zone Considerations
- Teams spanning multiple time zones should establish shared availability windows
- Meetings should be scheduled during overlapping business hours when possible
- Asynchronous communication (Slack, email) should be the default for non-urgent matters
- No expectation to respond to messages outside working hours

### 4.3 Calendar Management
Remote employees must:
- Keep Outlook calendars up to date with working hours and availability
- Block focus time for deep work (acceptable to decline meetings during focus blocks)
- Indicate "Working Remotely" status in Teams
- Set appropriate out-of-office messages for any planned unavailability

---

## 5. Communication Expectations

### 5.1 Response Time Standards
- **Slack/Teams messages:** Respond within 2 hours during working hours
- **Email:** Respond within 24 hours (acknowledge receipt if full response needs more time)
- **Urgent matters:** Use phone call or Teams call for immediate needs
- **Client communications:** Follow project-specific SLAs

### 5.2 Video Conferencing
- Camera should be on for team meetings, client calls, and 1:1s
- Camera-off is acceptable for large all-hands meetings, training sessions, and when bandwidth is an issue
- Use virtual backgrounds if home environment is not suitable for professional calls
- Mute when not speaking in meetings with 4+ participants

### 5.3 Daily Check-ins
Teams may establish daily stand-ups or check-in practices. These should be:
- Brief (15 minutes maximum)
- Focused on blockers and collaboration needs
- Respectful of time zones (rotate meeting times for distributed teams)

---

## 6. Performance and Accountability

### 6.1 Output-Based Evaluation
Remote employees are evaluated on output and results, not hours logged. Key metrics include:
- Quality of deliverables
- Meeting project deadlines
- Client satisfaction scores
- Collaboration effectiveness (peer feedback)
- Professional development progress

### 6.2 Regular Check-ins
Managers must conduct:
- Weekly 1:1 meetings (30 minutes minimum)
- Monthly performance conversations
- Quarterly goal reviews

### 6.3 Performance Concerns
If a remote employee's performance declines, the following steps apply:
1. Manager discusses concerns in 1:1
2. Written performance improvement plan (PIP) if needed
3. Possible requirement to return to office-based work during PIP
4. Standard performance management process applies

---

## 7. Health and Safety

### 7.1 Ergonomics
Remote employees are responsible for maintaining an ergonomic workspace. Perficient provides:
- Ergonomic assessment checklist (self-service)
- Virtual ergonomic consultation upon request
- Equipment recommendations and stipends

### 7.2 Workers' Compensation
Work-related injuries that occur in the home workspace during working hours are covered under Perficient's workers' compensation insurance. Employees must report injuries immediately to HR.

### 7.3 Mental Health
Perficient recognizes the isolation risks of remote work and provides:
- Employee Assistance Program (EAP): Free counseling sessions
- Virtual social events and coffee chats
- Wellness stipend ($50/month for gym, meditation apps, etc.)
- Encouraged use of PTO and mental health days

---

## 8. Security Requirements for Remote Work

### 8.1 Network Security
- Use Perficient VPN for all work activities
- Do not work from public Wi-Fi without VPN
- Keep home router firmware updated
- Use WPA2/WPA3 encryption

### 8.2 Physical Security
- Lock screen when stepping away
- Do not leave company devices unattended in public
- Store devices securely when not in use
- Shred sensitive printed documents

### 8.3 Data Protection
- Do not print confidential documents at home unless necessary
- Do not allow family members to use company devices
- Ensure video calls do not expose sensitive information (screen sharing awareness)
- Use approved cloud storage only (OneDrive, SharePoint)

---

## 9. Tax and Legal Considerations

### 9.1 Approved Work Locations
Perficient is registered to employ workers in specific states and countries. Employees must work from an approved location. Relocating to a new state/country requires HR approval minimum 30 days in advance.

### 9.2 Temporary Work from Another Location
Employees may work temporarily from a non-home location (e.g., visiting family) for up to 2 weeks without notification. Stays exceeding 2 weeks in another state require HR notification for tax compliance.

### 9.3 International Remote Work
Working from outside the United States requires:
- VP and Legal approval
- Maximum 4 weeks per calendar year
- Compliance with local labor laws and data regulations
- Awareness of data sovereignty requirements (some client data cannot leave the US)
