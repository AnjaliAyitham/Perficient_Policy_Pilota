# Perficient Code of Conduct

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024  
**Last Updated:** January 15, 2024  
**Policy Owner:** Chief Ethics & Compliance Officer  
**Applies To:** All Perficient employees, contractors, board members, and representatives

---

## 1. Our Commitment

Perficient is committed to conducting business with the highest standards of integrity, honesty, and ethical behavior. This Code of Conduct provides the framework for how we interact with clients, colleagues, partners, and the communities we serve.

---

## 2. Conflicts of Interest

### 2.1 Definition
A conflict of interest exists when personal interests interfere, or appear to interfere, with the interests of Perficient or its clients.

### 2.2 Examples
- Holding a financial interest in a competitor, client, or vendor
- Employing or contracting with family members without disclosure
- Using company resources for personal business ventures
- Serving on the board of a competitor or client without approval
- Romantic relationships with direct reports or clients

### 2.3 Disclosure Requirement
All potential conflicts must be disclosed in writing to your manager and the Ethics & Compliance team within 10 business days of becoming aware of the conflict. The Ethics team will evaluate and provide guidance.

---

## 3. Gifts and Entertainment

### 3.1 Receiving Gifts
- Gifts valued under $100: May be accepted without disclosure
- Gifts valued $100-$250: Must be disclosed to manager
- Gifts valued over $250: Must be declined or donated to Perficient's charity partner
- Cash or cash equivalents (gift cards): Never acceptable regardless of value
- Exception: Promotional items of nominal value (pens, mugs, t-shirts)

### 3.2 Giving Gifts
- Business gifts to clients/prospects must be approved by the account manager
- Maximum value: $150 per recipient per year
- No gifts to government officials without Legal approval
- All gifts must be recorded in the Gifts & Entertainment Registry

### 3.3 Entertainment
Reasonable business entertainment (meals, sporting events, concerts) is permitted when:
- There is a clear business purpose
- The expense is proportionate to the business relationship
- Both parties are present (no gift tickets without attendance)
- Total value does not exceed $300 per event per person

---

## 4. Confidentiality and Intellectual Property

### 4.1 Confidential Information
Employees must protect all confidential information including:
- Client business data, strategies, and systems
- Perficient financial data, forecasts, and strategic plans
- Employee personal information
- Proprietary methodologies and tools
- Pricing and contract terms

### 4.2 Non-Disclosure
Confidential information must never be disclosed to unauthorized parties, including:
- Family members
- Former colleagues at other companies
- Social media platforms
- Conference presentations (without Legal review)
- Industry publications

### 4.3 Intellectual Property
All work product created during employment belongs to Perficient. This includes code, designs, documentation, processes, and inventions conceived using company resources or related to company business.

---

## 5. Anti-Bribery and Anti-Corruption

### 5.1 Zero Tolerance
Perficient prohibits bribery and corruption in all forms. Employees must never:
- Offer, promise, or give anything of value to influence a business decision
- Accept bribes or kickbacks
- Make facilitation payments to government officials
- Use third parties to circumvent anti-bribery rules

### 5.2 Government Interactions
Special rules apply when interacting with government officials:
- No gifts, meals, or entertainment without Legal pre-approval
- Accurate record-keeping of all government interactions
- Compliance with the Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA) and UK Bribery Act

---

## 6. Social Media Guidelines

### 6.1 Personal Social Media
Employees may maintain personal social media accounts but must:
- Clearly state that views are their own, not Perficient's
- Never disclose confidential client or company information
- Not disparage clients, colleagues, or competitors
- Not use Perficient branding without Marketing approval
- Exercise caution discussing industry topics that could be attributed to Perficient

### 6.2 Official Perficient Communications
Only authorized employees (Marketing, PR, Executive Leadership) may make official statements on behalf of Perficient. Media inquiries must be directed to media@perficient.com.

### 6.3 Thought Leadership
Employees are encouraged to publish thought leadership content (blogs, articles, conference talks) with the following requirements:
- Content must not disclose confidential information
- Blog posts representing Perficient must be reviewed by Marketing
- Conference presentations require manager approval
- Use appropriate disclaimers when expressing personal opinions

---

## 7. Political Activities

### 7.1 Personal Political Activities
Employees may participate in political activities on their own time and with their own resources. Perficient does not:
- Endorse political candidates or parties
- Reimburse political contributions
- Allow use of company resources for political activities

### 7.2 Lobbying
Any lobbying activities on behalf of Perficient must be pre-approved by Legal and Government Affairs.

---

## 8. Environmental and Social Responsibility

### 8.1 Environmental Commitment
Perficient is committed to minimizing our environmental impact through:
- Carbon-neutral operations by 2027
- Paperless office initiatives
- Sustainable procurement practices
- Energy-efficient data center operations

### 8.2 Community Engagement
Perficient encourages employees to give back through:
- 16 hours of paid volunteer time per year
- Company-matched charitable donations (up to $1,000/year per employee)
- Pro-bono consulting for qualified nonprofits
- STEM education partnerships

---

## 9. Reporting Violations

### 9.1 Obligation to Report
All employees have a duty to report suspected violations of this Code, company policies, or applicable laws. Reports can be made through:
- Direct manager
- HR Business Partner
- Ethics & Compliance team (ethics@perficient.com)
- Anonymous Ethics Hotline: 1-800-PRFT-ETH (1-800-773-8384)
- Online portal: perficient.ethicspoint.com

### 9.2 Non-Retaliation
Perficient strictly prohibits retaliation against anyone who reports a concern in good faith. Retaliation includes termination, demotion, harassment, or any adverse employment action.

### 9.3 Investigation Process
All reports are investigated promptly and confidentially. The Ethics & Compliance team will:
1. Acknowledge receipt within 2 business days
2. Conduct a preliminary assessment
3. Investigate with appropriate confidentiality
4. Determine findings and recommend corrective action
5. Communicate outcomes to the reporter (to the extent permitted)

---

## 10. Consequences of Violations

Violations of this Code may result in disciplinary action up to and including:
- Verbal or written warning
- Required training or counseling
- Suspension without pay
- Demotion or reassignment
- Termination of employment
- Legal action and referral to authorities

The severity of consequences depends on the nature of the violation, whether it was intentional, the employee's history, and the impact on Perficient and its stakeholders.
